Margaret Hamilton

The woman who saved Apollo 11

Margaret Hamilton standing next to listings of the actual Apollo Guidance Computer (AGC) source code.

Here is a timeline of Margaret's life:



  • 1936 - Born in Paoli, Indiana
  • 1954 - Graduated from Hancock High School
  • 1958 - Graduated from Earlham College
  • 1960 - Received a software job from MIT
  • 1961 - 1963 - Worked on the SAGE Project at Lincoln Labs
  • 1963 - Joined the Charles Stark Draper Laboratory at MIT
  • 1963 - 1971 - Became the director and supervisor of software programming for Apollo and Skylab
  • 1969 - Accomplished the task of using software to land the first man on the moon
  • 1976 - 1984 - Became the CEO of Higher Order Software
  • 1986 - Became the founder and CEO of Hamilton Technologies, Inc.
  • 1986 - Received the Augusta Ada Lovelace Award
  • 2003 - Given the NASA Exceptional Space Act Award
  • 2009 - Received the Outstanding Alumni Award by Earlham College


    • “I hope,” Margaret says, “that we continue with exploration.”
